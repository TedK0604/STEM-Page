How We Did It

We used different materials to build a simple water filter and see how well it cleaned dirty water. These are the steps we followed:

  1. Measure 100g of Activated Carbon (charcoal)
  2. Prepare 50g of soil and 150 mL of water and mix (use a scale & graduated cylinder)
  3. Prepare the retort stand and connect it with the plastic bottle (acting as a filter funnel)
  4. Fill the plastic bottle with the activated carbon
  5. Place a 150ml beaker directly below the plastic bottle
  6. Pour the dirty water mixture into the plastic bottle (Keep stirring the mixture until the moment of pouring using a glass rod)
  7. Set the stopwatch and stop at 3 minutes
  8. Remove the beaker and plastic bottle
  9. Use the water purity tester to measure the electrical conductivity and the ppm (parts per million) of the purified watet
  10. Repeat steps 1 through 9 with different filter materials, respectively
